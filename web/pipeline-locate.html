Locate

How it works: a calibration tells lookout how a flat ground shape in the camera maps to real metres on the ground.

Child version: the camera photo is like a squashed drawing of the road. Calibration is the trick that unsquashes the drawing so we can use a ruler.

To collect points, take or upload a photo from the same fixed camera view. Put several known points on the same flat plane you want to measure. A ruler works for a close-up test: place it flat, click its carefully visible top corners or tick marks, and enter their real positions. For a road or path, use four or more known points spread across the ground plane.
