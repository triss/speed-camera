How this works

Capture and count crossings. lookout watches a fixed view, notices movement, and counts each time a moving blob crosses a line you draw. It stores timestamped observations and (if enabled) still JPEG images on this device. No cloud storage, no automated facial recognition, and no continuous footage leaves this phone.

This page expands on the basic counting tool by saving a snapshot of the camera at the exact moment a crossing is detected. You can review and page through these snapshots right in the browser using the Stills button.

  1. Press Start camera and aim at a stable spot.
  2. Tap two points to draw your crossing line.
  3. Once the line is placed, observing and capturing start automatically.
  4. Press the Stills button in the bottom HUD at any time to browse, filter, or review captured images and their metadata.

What is shared. Everything stays on this device. Measurements — counts, timestamps and direction — and, when enabled, a still image of each crossing are stored locally in the browser. Nothing leaves the phone automatically. When you tap Share Data, the CSV/JSON exports contain measurements only; the captured stills are included only if you explicitly choose Share Stills or Share Bundle. Turn image capture off any time with Capture still on crossing in Settings, and wipe everything with Clear local observations.

Stills are kept in the browser's IndexedDB storage. You can clear them at any time in the Share Data panel.
