lookout

Planet-first place observation

Use an old phone to understand a shared place

lookout watches one fixed view and records simple local observations: movement, visits, traffic, wildlife, and slow changes in the scene. Camera processing and storage stay on the device; sharing is explicit and uses findings or exported observation files, not footage by default.

It is meant for community questions that are too small, local, or too expensive for official monitoring: how fast cars pass a school gate, when a path gets busy, whether a garden is used by wildlife, or how often a low spot floods. These questions still matter to the people who live with them, but paying for specialist surveys, commercial cameras, or long-term monitoring can be out of reach. The phone stays pointed at the place, turns visual change into measurements, and keeps the raw material local unless someone chooses to share it.

Try one of the uses below. If the camera, fullscreen, storage, or frame rate gives you trouble, run the Capability check on the device you want to reuse. Each use has its own camera page, local observation log, and share buttons for CSV, JSON, and relevant stills.
